# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are OTTV camp participants (youth table tennis players) and their parents/followers at home in Switzerland. Participants need day-to-day camp facts on their phones: schedule, addresses, phrases, emergency contacts. Parents need reassurance and connection: public blog updates from coaches, plus a password-gated handbook with the same practical details. Coaches are secondary users who publish blog posts during the camp.

## Product Purpose

A dual-mode companion for the OTTV Tianjin 2026 training camp: a public blog that shares the journey with followers, and a private password-gated handbook with internal camp information. Success means parents feel informed without chasing WhatsApp threads, and players can quickly find the next address, phrase, or emergency number in China.

## Brand Personality

focused · trustworthy · playfully adventurous

Voice is clear and practical first, then lets curiosity and sport energy show. Slight bounce in wording and motion — never childish, never corporate. Emotions: confidence for parents, excitement for players, respect for the training context with Ma Wenge.

## Anti-references

- Generic sports-club WordPress templates with stock medal clipart
- Purple-gradient SaaS landing pages
- Dense PDF-as-webpage info dumps
- Dark “esports” neon dashboards
- Overly official federation bureaucracy aesthetics

## Design Principles

1. **Two clear modes, one brand** — Public and private share identity; switching should feel intentional, not like two different websites.
2. **Glanceable for camp life** — Critical info (schedule, addresses, contacts) wins over decoration when someone is tired or mid-travel.
3. **Story for the followers** — The public blog carries the adventure; the private area carries the logistics.
4. **Trust through clarity** — Emergency and travel facts are plain, accurate, and easy to find — no scavenger hunts.
5. **Playful, not silly** — Energy comes from motion, type, and accent color; copy stays coach-credible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1 (prefer ≥7:1). Support `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keep touch targets large for phones. German is the primary language; Chinese addresses and phrases stay readable with clear labels (Pinyin + characters).
